Research vision

Risk intelligence for uncertain physical and financial systems.

My work connects stochastic geotechnics, artificial intelligence, and decision-oriented risk analysis. The central question is simple: when data are sparse, noisy, heterogeneous, or shifting over time, how can we still make reliable predictions and better decisions?

The lab builds models that integrate physical mechanisms, spatial-temporal randomness, machine learning, and interpretable uncertainty measures for engineering, energy, and finance applications.

01

AI-informed random fields

Learning spatial heterogeneity from sparse observations, CPT data, images, and numerical simulations to model subsurface uncertainty.

02

Geohazard & infrastructure risk

Physics-informed simulation and uncertainty propagation for landslides, underground structures, slopes, and resilient infrastructure.

03

Energy and financial risk

Risk pricing, tail-event modelling, stochastic processes, and AI-assisted decision systems for energy transfer and trading contexts.

Selected project directions

Bridging engineering risk, AI, and finance.

Spatio-temporal random fields for geotechnical systems

AI-assisted generation, updating, and interpretation of non-stationary random fields for soil and rock heterogeneity.

Stochastic material point method for landslide risk

Uncertainty-aware simulation of debris avalanche initiation, runout, and consequence under complex terrain and material variability.

Computer vision for crack propagation and rock mechanics

Image segmentation, perception models, and physical interpretation for geotechnical image analysis and failure mechanisms.

Energy risk transfer and computational finance

Hybrid CAT bond pricing, risk transfer structures, option-style thinking, and data-driven pricing for energy and climate-related risk.

Teaching & supervision

Mathematics, engineering judgement, and research-led learning.

I teach Engineering Mathematics and supervise student projects across civil engineering, applied mathematics, computer science, blockchain, AI, and risk analysis. My teaching aims to help students move from formula-based learning to model-based reasoning.
